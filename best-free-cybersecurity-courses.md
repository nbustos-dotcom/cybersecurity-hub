# Best Free Cybersecurity Courses in 2026 (Ranked by a CS Student)

I'm a Computer Science student at Michigan Tech with a cybersecurity minor,
and I also self-study outside of class. I've gone through dozens of free
cybersecurity resources — here's what actually works and what's a waste
of time.

## The Short List (If You're in a Hurry)

| Platform | Best For | Cost |
|----------|----------|------|
| TryHackMe | Total beginners | Free tier available |
| HackTheBox Academy | Intermediate learners | Free modules |
| Professor Messer (YouTube) | Cert prep (Security+) | Free |
| Cybrary | Broad course library | Free tier |
| OverTheWire | Linux/wargame practice | Free |
| SANS Cyber Aces | Foundations | Free |
| Google Cybersecurity Certificate | Career starters | Free on audit |

## 1. TryHackMe — Best for Absolute Beginners

TryHackMe is where I send everyone who asks me how to get started. The
guided learning paths make it impossible to get lost, and the browser-based
attack box means you can practice without setting up a local VM.

### Best Free Paths on TryHackMe
- **Pre-Security** — covers networking, Linux, and web fundamentals
- **Introduction to Cybersecurity** — broad overview of the field
- **Jr Penetration Tester** — hands-on intro to pentesting

### What I Like
- Zero setup required — everything runs in the browser
- Hints available when you're genuinely stuck
- Active community and Discord

### What I Don't Like
- Some rooms feel too guided — you're not really problem solving
- Free tier has daily machine limits

**Verdict:** Start here. Spend your first 2-3 months on TryHackMe before
moving anywhere else.

[Start TryHackMe Free →](https://tryhackme.com)

## 2. HackTheBox Academy — Best for Intermediate Learners

Once TryHackMe has given you the basics, HackTheBox Academy is where you
level up. The free modules are genuinely high quality — not watered down
versions of paid content.

### Best Free Modules
- Introduction to Networking
- Linux Fundamentals
- Web Requests
- Introduction to Web Applications

### What I Like
- Much more realistic than TryHackMe
- Free modules are comprehensive
- Directly maps to OSCP prep

### What I Don't Like
- Steep jump from beginner content
- Some essential modules are paid

**Verdict:** Move here after 2-3 months on TryHackMe.

[Start HackTheBox Academy Free →](https://academy.hackthebox.com)

## 3. Professor Messer — Best Free Cert Prep (YouTube)

If you're studying for CompTIA Security+, Professor Messer's free YouTube
course is all you need. I used it alongside my university coursework and
it filled in every gap.

### What's Available Free
- Complete Security+ course (SY0-701) on YouTube
- Free study groups
- Practice questions on his site

**Verdict:** If you're targeting Security+, watch this entire playlist before
buying any paid course.

[Professor Messer on YouTube →](https://youtube.com/@professormesser)

## 4. OverTheWire — Best for Linux Practice

OverTheWire is a series of wargames that teach Linux and security concepts
through puzzles. Bandit (the first wargame) is perfect for Linux beginners.

I worked through Bandit during my first semester and it made everything
else click faster.

**Verdict:** Do Bandit alongside TryHackMe's Pre-Security path.

[Start OverTheWire Free →](https://overthewire.org)

## 5. Cybrary — Best Broad Course Library

Cybrary has hundreds of cybersecurity courses on their free tier. Quality
varies but the SOC Analyst and Penetration Testing paths are solid.

**Verdict:** Good supplement to TryHackMe. Don't use it as your primary
resource.

[Start Cybrary Free →](https://cybrary.it)

## 6. Google Cybersecurity Certificate — Best for Career Starters

Google's Cybersecurity Certificate on Coursera is normally paid but you
can audit it for free. It's designed for complete beginners targeting
SOC Analyst roles.

**Verdict:** Great if you're targeting a SOC Analyst role as your first job.
Audit it for free — don't pay.

## Setting Up Your Practice Lab (Free)

All of these courses are better with a hands-on lab. Here's what I use:

### Free Local Setup
- VirtualBox (free) — runs virtual machines on your laptop
- Kali Linux (free) — standard pentesting OS
- Metasploitable (free) — intentionally vulnerable VM to practice on

### Cloud Lab Option
If your laptop can't handle VMs, spin up a cheap VPS instead:
- [DigitalOcean](https://m.do.co/c/a2644c2e88b4) — $4/mo, plus $200 free
  credits for new users
- Install Kali or Ubuntu and practice from anywhere

## My Recommended Learning Order

1. **Month 1:** TryHackMe Pre-Security + OverTheWire Bandit
2. **Month 2-3:** TryHackMe Jr Penetration Tester path
3. **Month 3-4:** HackTheBox Academy free modules
4. **Month 4-6:** Professor Messer Security+ + practice exams
5. **Month 6+:** HackTheBox machines + CTF competitions

## Final Thoughts

The best free cybersecurity education available today is genuinely better
than what most paid bootcamps offered 5 years ago. TryHackMe and HackTheBox
Academy alone can take you from zero to job-ready if you're consistent.

The only thing that separates people who make it from those who don't is
showing up every day — even if it's just 30 minutes.
