# Top 10 CTF Platforms for Beginners in 2026

Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions and practice platforms are the best
way to build real cybersecurity skills. As a CS student at Michigan Tech
who's worked through multiple platforms, here are the ones actually worth
your time.

## What is a CTF?

A CTF (Capture The Flag) is a cybersecurity challenge where you find hidden
"flags" — usually a string of text like `flag{this_is_the_flag}` — by
exploiting vulnerabilities, solving puzzles, or reversing code.

They're the fastest way to build practical skills because you're actually
hacking, not just watching videos.

## The Top 10 CTF Platforms

### 1. TryHackMe — Best for Absolute Beginners

TryHackMe is the most beginner-friendly platform available. Instead of
throwing you into challenges cold, it guides you through learning paths
that teach concepts before testing them.

**Why it's #1 for beginners:**
- Guided learning paths with built-in hints
- Browser-based attack machine — no setup needed
- Huge library of beginner rooms
- Active community and Discord

**Best free rooms to start:**
- Introduction to Cybersecurity
- Pre-Security path
- Jr Penetration Tester path

**Cost:** Free tier available, Premium ~$14/mo

[Start TryHackMe Free →](https://tryhackme.com)

---

### 2. HackTheBox — Best for Intermediate Learners

HackTheBox is where you go after TryHackMe gives you the foundations.
Machines are realistic, challenging, and much closer to real penetration
testing scenarios.

**Why it's #2:**
- Extremely realistic machines
- Active community with writeups after solving
- Pro Labs for advanced learners
- Great for OSCP preparation

**Best free starting point:**
- Starting Point machines (guided, beginner friendly)
- HackTheBox Academy free modules

**Cost:** Free tier available, VIP ~$14/mo

[Start HackTheBox Free →](https://hackthebox.com)

---

### 3. PicoCTF — Best Free CTF for Students

PicoCTF is run by Carnegie Mellon University and designed specifically
for students. It's completely free and has hundreds of challenges across
all difficulty levels.

**Why it's great:**
- 100% free forever
- Designed for students and beginners
- Covers web, crypto, forensics, reverse engineering, and binary
- Challenges stay live after competitions end

**Cost:** Completely free

[Start PicoCTF →](https://picoctf.org)

---

### 4. OverTheWire — Best for Linux and Web Basics

OverTheWire wargames are free challenges that teach Linux and security
concepts through increasingly difficult puzzles. Bandit (the first
wargame) is perfect for Linux beginners.

**Best wargames in order:**
1. Bandit — Linux basics
2. Natas — Web security basics
3. Leviathan — Linux privilege escalation
4. Krypton — Cryptography basics

**Cost:** Completely free

[Start OverTheWire →](https://overthewire.org)

---

### 5. CTFtime.org — Find Live CTF Competitions

CTFtime isn't a practice platform — it's a calendar of every live CTF
competition happening worldwide. Competing in live CTFs is the best way
to level up fast because you're solving fresh challenges under time
pressure.

**How to use it:**
- Filter by difficulty (beginner friendly)
- Join as a solo player or form a team
- Read writeups after competitions end

**Cost:** Free

[Browse CTF competitions →](https://ctftime.org)

---

### 6. VulnHub — Free Downloadable Vulnerable VMs

VulnHub hosts hundreds of free downloadable virtual machines that are
intentionally vulnerable. Download them, run them in VirtualBox, and
practice attacking them offline.

**Why it's great:**
- Completely free and offline
- Huge variety of machines
- Great for practicing with your own lab setup
- Many machines have walkthroughs available

**You'll need:**
- VirtualBox (free)
- At least 8GB RAM

[Browse VulnHub machines →](https://vulnhub.com)

---

### 7. Root Me — Large Free Challenge Library

Root Me has over 400 free challenges across web, network, forensics,
cryptography, and more. French platform but fully available in English.

**Why it's worth trying:**
- Huge free challenge library
- Covers many categories TryHackMe doesn't
- Good for breadth of knowledge

**Cost:** Free tier, Pro available

[Start Root Me →](https://root-me.org)

---

### 8. Cyber Defenders — Best for Blue Team/SOC Skills

Most platforms focus on offensive security. Cyber Defenders focuses on
defensive skills — log analysis, threat hunting, incident response.
Essential if you're targeting a SOC Analyst role.

**Best free labs:**
- Intro to Endpoint Security
- Threat Intelligence fundamentals
- Log analysis challenges

**Cost:** Free tier available

[Start Cyber Defenders →](https://cyberdefenders.org)

---

### 9. SANS Holiday Hack Challenge — Best Annual Free Event

SANS runs a free holiday-themed CTF every December that's become a
beloved tradition in the security community. Challenges are creative,
beginner-friendly, and prizes are awarded.

**Why it's worth knowing:**
- Completely free
- Excellent production quality
- Great community event
- Past challenges stay available year-round

**Cost:** Free

[SANS Holiday Hack →](https://holidayhackchallenge.com)

---

### 10. Pwn.college — Best for Advanced Binary Exploitation

Pwn.college is run by Arizona State University and focuses on low-level
binary exploitation and system security. It's harder than everything else
on this list but completely free and exceptionally well designed.

**Best for:**
- Students with programming background
- Anyone targeting advanced pentesting roles
- Binary exploitation and reverse engineering

**Cost:** Completely free

[Start Pwn.college →](https://pwn.college)

## Which Platform Should You Start With?

| Experience Level | Start Here |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Complete beginner | TryHackMe |
| Know Linux basics | PicoCTF or OverTheWire |
| Comfortable with basics | HackTheBox Starting Point |
| Want offline practice | VulnHub |
| Targeting SOC roles | Cyber Defenders |
| Want live competitions | CTFtime.org |

## Setting Up Your Practice Environment

For platforms that need a local setup (VulnHub etc.) you'll need:
- VirtualBox (free) for running VMs locally
- Or a [DigitalOcean VPS](https://m.do.co/c/a2644c2e88b4) for cloud
  practice — new users get $200 free credits

For TryHackMe and HackTheBox you just need a browser — no setup required.

## My Personal Recommendation

**Start with TryHackMe for 2-3 months**, then add HackTheBox. Supplement
with PicoCTF challenges and OverTheWire Bandit for extra practice. Once
you're comfortable join a live CTF on CTFtime.org — even if you only solve
one challenge it's a great experience.

The cybersecurity community is genuinely welcoming to beginners. Don't be
afraid to ask for help in Discord servers and subreddits.
