The Free 12-Week Study Plan

Weeks 1-4: Core Content

Professor Messer's complete Security+ SY0-701 course is on YouTube and his website — completely free. This is the single best free resource for Security+ and covers every exam objective.

  • Watch 1-2 hours per day
  • Take notes on every section
  • Re-watch anything you don't understand

Primary resource: Professor Messer Security+ Course — completely free, covers every exam objective.

Weeks 5-8: Deep Dive by Domain

Focus extra time on Security Operations (28%) — it's the biggest domain and heavily tested.

Weeks 9-10: Practice Tests

Practice tests are the most important part of exam prep. Do not skip this.

  • Professor Messer's practice exams (some free on his site)
  • ExamCompass.com — free Security+ practice questions
  • Reddit r/CompTIA — community shares free resources regularly

Target score: Get consistently above 80% on practice tests before booking the real exam.

Weeks 11-12: Review and Weak Areas

  • Identify your weakest domains from practice tests
  • Re-watch Professor Messer sections for those domains
  • Do final practice exams daily
  • Review your notes

Performance Based Questions (PBQs)

PBQs are the hardest part of Security+ for most people. They're hands-on simulations where you have to actually configure something or analyze a scenario.

How to prepare:

  • Set up a free lab with VirtualBox and practice configuring firewalls and access controls
  • TryHackMe rooms give hands-on practice that directly helps with PBQs
  • DigitalOcean VPS — $200 free credits if your PC can't run local VMs

Exam Day Tips

  • Read every question twice before answering
  • Flag uncertain questions and come back
  • For PBQs — skip them first, do all multiple choice, then return
  • Trust your preparation — don't second guess yourself
  • Arrive 15 minutes early if taking at a testing center

After You Pass

  1. eJPT — practical pentesting cert, great for hands-on roles
  2. CompTIA CySA+ — if you want a SOC/analyst career path
  3. OSCP — if you want to go deep into penetration testing
